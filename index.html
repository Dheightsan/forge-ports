Licenses & rights

I only port what I have rights to port

Most devs in this space ignore licensing. I don't — because a DMCA takedown or account ban hurts you, not just me.

Public — open-source & compatibility

Open-source mods (MIT · Apache · LGPL), mods with written permission, and compatibility add-ons (my own code, depending on mods you install yourself) get published to CurseForge & Modrinth — full credit to original authors.

Private — closed-source mods

Most requested mods are closed-source. You bring one you have the right to use; I modify it for you and deliver privately — to you only, never published. You pay for my time, not for redistribution.

What I won't do

Publicly redistribute a closed-source (All Rights Reserved) mod without written author permission. That's the one line I never cross — it protects your accounts from takedowns and bans.

The principle: You pay for my time writing the work — not for distribution rights. The right to publish always belongs to whoever holds the license. That's the only clean, legally sound way to run a porting service.
